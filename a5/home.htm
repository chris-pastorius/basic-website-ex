The JavaJam website uses hierarchical organization.
It is the most appropriate organization for the site because it offers simple navigation for the easygoing nature of the site.
It doesn't have an overabundance of pages, so to convert it to something linear or random would be inappropriate.

Question 2:

Color and graphics have been well-implemented on the JavaJam website, sticking to three or four colors. The images aren't distracting in the least.
Navigation in the form of a simple navigation bar at the top of the page is a highlight of the website that has been well-done.
Functionality is another aspect that has been implemented expertly, all of the links work as intended.

In terms of Page Layout, the margins being set so there's so much blank space on each side of the page is vaguely tacky
and could be set so the page fills the screen.
The website as of now feels very inaccesible to those with disabilites.
The website is not greatly designed for the mobile experience.
Other than those, I think it's a pretty standard website, it just needs little improvment.
